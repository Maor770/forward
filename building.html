The Vision: A permanent Maor headquarters and visitor center in Crown Heights — a place where children will experience the power and beauty of the Rebbe's Sichos.

What the visitor center will offer
  • Interactive exhibits that bring the Rebbe's Sichos to life — through sights, sounds, and stories
  • Immersive experiences for children and families — hands-on, age-appropriate, deeply Chassidish
  • Live programs tied to the Jewish calendar
  • A welcoming home for families, schools, camps, and visitors from around the world
Three roles, one home

A professional production home for our growing team. A destination where children and families engage with Maor and with the Rebbe's Sichos. A strategic real estate position that appreciates while rental income carries the operation.

The Phased Occupancy Strategy

An income-producing property where Maor expands progressively as leases turn over. Year 1: tenants in place, Maor in basement/garage. Year 2-3: first turnover, primary studio comes online. Year 4-5: full Maor occupation — with the visitor center fully realized: interactive exhibits where children meet the Rebbe's Sichos, immersive experiences for families, live programs throughout the year, and a welcoming home for visitors from around the world.

$1.75M Building Allocation Breakdown

The $1.75M allocation covers four components of acquisition and stabilization:

  • Down Payment - 20–35% of purchase price depending on selected property and lender terms
  • Closing Costs - ~5% of purchase price: title, transfer tax, attorney, escrow, mortgage tax
  • Renovation & Buildout - Full renovation of the property plus Maor's buildout (basement studio, garage conversion, showcase and activity hall, tenant retention upgrades)
  • Operating Buffer - 12-month reserve for vacancy, repairs, or tenant transitions through year-one stabilization
Cash Flow Philosophy

Rental income covers all or most of debt service, depending on the property selected. Where rental falls short, Maor's operating budget bridges the gap as we phase into ownership. The asset itself is the value driver.

Mortgage Strategy
  • Target LTV: 65–80% depending on property and DSCR profile
  • Target rate: Commercial multifamily market (~6.5%), locked at the closing window's best point
  • Loan type: Commercial multifamily, 30-year amortization, community lender options where available
5-Year Cash Flow Model

Two scenarios per property: Base case (planned upside executed, vacancy at market norm 5%) and Conservative case (no upside, vacancy at 10%). Both scenarios show the property remains operational.

Methodology

Every property is reviewed with the full team - brokers, advisors, and lenders together. Financing options are explored across multiple sources, including friends of Maor offering mission-aligned terms. No property advances to Letter of Intent without team sign-off.

Current Status

Documentation actively in formation. Property lists arriving regularly from our broker team. Mortgage pre-qualifications underway with several lenders. All legal acquisition pathways under review - including bargain sale structures, where the seller donates a portion of the property value in exchange for a tax benefit. Ready for contract review at LOI.

The heart of the process. A 4-parameter scoring system gives every property a weighted total - measurable criteria, not gut feel. Only properties scoring 70+ advance to deep due diligence; 80+ to Letter of Intent.

The Four Parameters & Weights
  • Operations & Cash-Flow Ratio (25 pts): Whether existing rental income covers monthly debt service. Measured by Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) - at typical down-payment ratios for the property.
  • Strategic Location on Growth Vector (25 pts): Walking distance to 770, proximity to anchor Anash institutions, and position on Crown Heights' eastern growth corridor.
  • Property Development Potential (20 pts): Unused FAR and zoning flexibility, basement and rooftop optionality, air rights for future vertical expansion.
  • Alignment with Maor's Needs (30 pts): Suitability for Maor offices, a showcase and activity hall for children and families, and phased Maor occupancy over 5 years.
Decision Threshold

70/100 minimum to proceed to deep due diligence. 80/100 minimum to submit Letter of Intent. Below 70 - declined. The team's time is the scarce resource, not the property pool.

Year 1 - Stabilization

Acquire and stabilize. Existing leases honored, pre-close upside terms executed. Maor occupies basement, garage, or non-residential space. Property fully tenanted from day one.

Year 2-3 - First Conversion

First major lease ends — convert that unit to Maor's primary studio and the first phase of the visitor center. Remaining tenant continues; partial rental income preserved.

Year 4-5 - Full Operation

Second lease conversion. By year 5, Maor occupies the full operational footprint — offices, production studios, and the fully realized visitor center: interactive exhibits where children meet the Rebbe's Sichos, immersive experiences for families, and live programs throughout the year.

Decision Trigger Points
  • Lease end date: Each lease's natural expiration triggers conversion review
  • Maor headcount milestone: When the team grows beyond current capacity, pull conversion forward
  • Funding triggers: Conversion funded from Maor operating budget + targeted donor renovation gifts
Maor Space Mapping (Years 1-5)

Year 1: basement → AI workspace and render farm. Year 3: first converted unit → Maor offices, writer's room, and the first visitor center exhibits. Year 5: second converted unit → the full visitor center — interactive exhibits, immersive experiences, screening room, and event space for children and families. The building grows with Maor; Maor grows into the building.

Ownership Structure

Based on our research, we are considering acquisition through a single-member LLC wholly owned by Maor's 501(c)(3) parent organization - the standard structure for nonprofit-held real estate. This separates real-estate liability from program operations, allows faster mortgage approval, and preserves tax treatment on Maor-occupied portions.

Tax-Exempt Acquisition

Acquisition as a tax-exempt property is the simpler and more likely path. Once Maor occupies a portion of the building, that portion is held without property tax - meaningful operational savings as Maor's footprint grows. Three legal pathways under active review:

  • RPTL §420-a - New York State property tax exemption for portions occupied by the not-for-profit. Likely applicable as Maor's footprint grows.
  • IRS 501(c)(2) Title-Holding Model - alternative structure for a full title-holding subsidiary; final structure determined by counsel.
  • Contemplated Use Exemption - during transition years, portions formally designated for future Maor use may qualify for partial exemption under New York State law.
Zoning

Crown Heights zoning generally permits not-for-profit and community uses in residential areas, alongside residential and small-commercial use. Each candidate property is reviewed for its specific zoning, build potential, and approval pathway before LOI.

Open Items for Legal Review
  • Final LLC operating agreement structure (single-member vs. multi-member, for future flexibility)
  • Property-specific lease assumption analysis at LOI
  • Closing-stage tax assessor coordination - re-assessment timing and exemption application
Donor Protections

Donations are received through Maor's 501(c)(3) and used in accordance with applicable law. Every donor receives a standard tax-deductible receipt.

Transparency on risk is part of the discipline of asking for capital. The honest assessment - risk by risk, with mitigation.

Property-Specific Risks
  • DOB / HPD Violations - Open violations or unpermitted construction can materially affect the property. Mitigation: Phase I public records search; flagged properties require a clear resolution path before LOI.
  • Hidden Structural Issues - Even good-condition properties can hide expensive surprises. Mitigation: Licensed structural engineer review before LOI on every property.
  • Tenant Hostility / Holdover Risk - Some tenants may resist new ownership or lease changes. Mitigation: Tenant interviews during diligence; legal counsel on transition; budget includes retention upgrades (kitchen/bath) where strategic.
Market & Macroeconomic Risks
  • Interest Rate Movement - Closing-window rates affect debt service. Mitigation: Multiple lender quotes locked simultaneously; rate locked at the window's best point. Stress-tested at +100bp.
  • Appraisal Below Asking - A low appraisal reduces the loan amount. Mitigation: Conservative LTV scenarios modeled; renegotiation pathway pre-agreed with seller; campaign reserve covers additional equity if needed.
  • Neighborhood Pace - The strategic case rests on Crown Heights continuing its growth trajectory. Mitigation: The property is income-producing - rental carries it regardless of pace, independent of appreciation.
Timeline - First 120 Days
  • Days 1-30: Pipeline expansion, deep diligence on top candidates. Mortgage pre-qualifications finalized.
  • Days 30-60: Top property selected for LOI. Comprehensive Phase II diligence on chosen property. Lender selection and term sheet negotiation.
  • Days 60-90: Letter of Intent submitted. Phase III diligence (legal, zoning, structural). Contract negotiation and execution.
  • Days 90-120: Closing process. Title transfer. Year 1 of phased occupancy begins.
Walk-Away Triggers

We will pause the entire selection process - not just one property - if: (a) consecutive properties fail diligence with similar issues, suggesting a market signal we hadn't read; (b) lender terms across the board break the financial model; (c) a major regulatory change materially shifts the framework. The campaign isn't "buy a building this year." It is "make the right decision for Maor."
